At Nebyss, we start from a simple conviction: the most dangerous moment in AI development is when an engineer believes the model knows better than the domain expert.

## The Automation Trap

Generic AI models are powerful. They can surface patterns across enormous datasets, flag anomalies in milliseconds, and generate outputs that look authoritative. But in critical domains — energy, public policy, healthcare — that authority is borrowed, not earned.

When you remove the expert from the loop, you don't just lose accuracy. You lose accountability. You lose the ability to ask "why did this decision get made?" in a way that anyone affected can understand.

## What Human-Supervised Intelligence Looks Like

Our approach centers on what we call **Impact-Aware AI Pipelines**. Every stage of the pipeline is designed around three questions:

1. **Who is affected by this output?** Not just the immediate user, but downstream stakeholders who may have no visibility into the decision.
2. **Can a domain expert challenge this?** The model should surface confidence intervals, conflicting signals, and edge cases — not suppress them.
3. **Is the output traceable?** From input data to final recommendation, every step should be auditable.

This isn't a technical constraint. It's a design philosophy.

## Why Domain Specificity Matters

A model trained on general medical literature will confidently recommend treatments that a cardiologist would immediately flag as inappropriate for a specific patient profile. The confidence gap between what the model "knows" and what it actually understands is where real-world harm lives.

Our work in the energy sector showed us this firsthand. Grid optimization models trained on European averages performed poorly the moment you introduced the specific regulatory constraints, legacy infrastructure, and operator preferences of a single country. Specificity isn't a feature — it's the foundation.

## Building Differently

We're early. We say so openly. But we're building Atractos — our domain intelligence platform — from this philosophy outward, not bolting human oversight on at the end.

The result, we believe, will be AI that domain experts actually trust. Not because it's constrained, but because it's honest about what it knows and what it doesn't.
